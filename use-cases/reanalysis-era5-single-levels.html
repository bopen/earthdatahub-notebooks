Now, we want to compute the average October 2023 temperature on an area comprising the entire territory of Germany.

To do so, we'll perform the following steps:

  • Access the "2 metre temperature" variable and convert it from K to Celsius;
  • Restrict the data to a limited rectangular area (Germany);
  • Select only data belonging to a specific month (October 2023);
  • Compute the time average of the selected data.
In [4]:
%%time

temperature = ds.t2m - 273.15
temperature.attrs["units"] = "C"
temperature_area = temperature.sel(**area)
temperature_area_month = temperature_area.sel(valid_time="2023-10")
temperature_area_month_mean = temperature_area_month.mean(dim="valid_time").compute()

t2m =  ds.t2m
t2m_loc = t2m.sel(**location, method="nearest")
t2m_climatology_mean = t2m_loc.sel(valid_time=reference_period).groupby("valid_time.month").mean(dim="valid_time").compute()
t2m_climatology_std = t2m_loc.sel(valid_time=reference_period).groupby("valid_time.month").std(dim="valid_time").compute()
t2m_loc_monthly_means = t2m_loc.sel(valid_time="2023").resample(valid_time="1M").mean(dim="valid_time").compute()
